Biography

Zheng He is an MD and PhD candidate in cardiology at Peking University First Hospital and Peking University Health Science Center. His research focuses on hypertension, familial hypercholesterolemia, lipid metabolism, and cardiometabolic diseases. Methodologically, he applies Mendelian randomization, genetic epidemiology, whole-exome sequencing, and advanced statistical modelling to dissect causal pathways between drug targets, intermediate phenotypes, and cardiovascular and neurological outcomes.

Currently, he is particularly interested in leveraging whole-exome sequencing data from (suspected) FH cases in Chinese populations to refine variant interpretation, improve risk stratification, and contribute to the development of an updated guideline/consensus for FH. In addition, he is involved in work exploring non-LDLR functional mechanisms of PCSK9, aiming to better understand how PCSK9 influences lipid metabolism and cardiovascular risk beyond classical LDL receptor pathways.

During his master's training in cardiovascular medicine at South China University of Technology, he worked on community-based cohorts and large-scale genetic datasets to study walking pace, antihypertensive drug target genes, gut microbiota, and risks of heart failure, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and Alzheimer's disease. His first-author work has been published in Nutrition, Metabolism and Cardiovascular Diseases, Alzheimer's Research & Therapy, the Journal of Clinical Hypertension, and the Journal of Stroke and Cerebrovascular Diseases.

Clinically, he is a licensed physician in internal medicine (cardiology) and aims to bridge evidence from genetic and omics studies with precision prevention and treatment strategies for high-risk cardiovascular patients.

Research Interests

Research Focus

  • Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH) - Leveraging whole-exome sequencing data from Chinese populations to refine variant interpretation, improve risk stratification, and inform updated diagnostic and management guidelines for FH
  • PCSK9 Beyond LDLR - Investigating non-LDLR functional mechanisms of PCSK9, including its effects on vascular inflammation, thrombosis, and immune regulation, with implications for optimizing lipid-lowering strategies and reducing ASCVD risk in high-risk populations
  • Hypertension & Cardiometabolic Diseases - Applying genetic and epidemiological approaches to understand how blood pressure, lipid disorders, and genetic variants influence cardiovascular and cerebrovascular disease outcomes

Methodological Interests

  • Mendelian randomization - Two-sample, multivariable, bidirectional, and mediation MR; drug-target MR to proxy antihypertensive drug classes and target gene expression
  • Genetic epidemiology & biostatistics - GWAS summary data analysis (e.g., MEGASTROKE, FinnGen, UK Biobank and other large consortia); survival analysis, longitudinal models, meta-analysis, and risk prediction
  • Clinical and community-based cohort studies - Design and analysis of hospital-based and community-based cohort studies in cardiology; data collection, cleaning, and management using R; construction of analysis-ready datasets; application of longitudinal and time-to-event models to real-world data on hypertension, heart failure, and other cardiometabolic outcomes
  • Omics & sequencing - Variant annotation and prioritization in whole-exome sequencing, with a focus on FH- and lipid-related genes; integration of genetic, clinical, and microbiome data

Programming & Tools

  • R - tidyverse, data.table, TwoSampleMR, MendelianRandomization, metafor, survival
  • Python - pandas, NumPy, SciPy, scikit-learn, plotting libraries
  • Bioinformatics tools - MR-Base platform, PLINK, basic shell scripting
  • Reproducible research - R Markdown / Quarto, Jupyter notebooks
